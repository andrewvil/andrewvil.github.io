WHY ARE HORROR GAMES SO SCARY?


*Freddy from Five Night's at Freddy's

Horror games have been one of the most popular game genres.

Celebrities like Pewdiepie, Markiplier, Jacksepiceye have documented playing many horror games, which contributed to it's popularity. However, without its high-quality immersive experience, it would not be where it is today.

Horror game developers keep these things in mind:

STORY

The most effective way to immerse the player before even playing the game.

*The Mortuary Assistant

In the game The Mortuary Assistant, the story starts off with an apprentice mortician that has to work alone in a mortuary that has a reputation for supernatural events.

With that, the player already knows to expect having to deal with those events, and the idea of a haunted mortuary sounds scary.

*Five Nights at Freddy's 3 game.

Many horror games love to use darkness.
This takes advantage of our evolutionary fear of darkness.

Darkness also comes with an unknown factor, as it gives players absence of information.

That leaves the players unsettled and guessing for answers.

Would you have known there was a paper bin on the bottom left corner?


Horror games like to make use of uncanny visuals to add to the unsettling feeling

click the image to unblur/blur it.




*Omori mirror easter egg



It is best to choose uncanny over realism, as audience love to scrutinize attempted realism and that can distract them from the immersion. Being clearly unrealistic throws the idea of attempted realism out the window.
